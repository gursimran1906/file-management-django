HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

 

Your Obligations in Outline

An employer which has not previously taken steps to comply with its obligations under health and safety law should proceed as follows:

  • Prepare a health and safety policy, send it to everyone and put it on the intranet or noticeboard. Also tell people that there is some mandatory training coming.
  • Carry out health and safety risks assessments and implement the results.
  • Provide training.
  • Keep records.

 

How to Proceed

The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) website has a huge amount of reference material and guidance on your obligations and appropriate precautions (http://www.hse.gov.uk/). The person responsible in your organisation should visit that site. S/he should then prepare your health and safety policy and carry out a risk assessment. If you have five employees or more these must be in writing.

Following the risk assessment you must do the following.

  • Make arrangements for implementing the safety measures identified as necessary by the risk assessment (for example that will normally include holding a fire drill at least annually and having a system for workstation assessments). See below for notes on what are likely to be the main areas of concern.
  • Appoint competent people (e.g. responsible support staff) to help implement the arrangements.
  • Provide clear information and training to employees.
  • If relevant, work together with other employers which share the same workplace.

This document does not duplicate the helpful precedents and information on the HSE site. However below is an example of what a health and safety policy may look like for a typical firm. Also see the separate Socrates precedent Health and Safety Risk Assessment precedent.

Firms vary, so these precedents must not be merely copied. You should go through the risk factors itemised on the HSE website and apply them to your firm.

It is good practice for the senior management of the firm to make a statement emphasising its commitment to health and safety. This may appear in an email in which you circulate your policy to all staff. Alternatively it may appear as a prologue to the policy itself. See the “Commitment Statement” example below.

Large firms, or those which have unusual risks, should consider engaging a specialist consultant. The Main Areas to Address

In a typical office the main risk areas will be these.

  • Workstation assessments: In respect of workers who regularly use “display screen equipment” (computer screens etc) as a significant part of their normal work (that is daily, for continuous periods of an hour or more) you must:
    • carry out workstation assessments (see the HSE questionnaire at http://www.hse.gov.uk/pubns/ck1.pdf which you can use for this purpose; alternatively the Socrates training module includes a questionnaire users can complete which also meets this need);
    • make sure controls are in place, and provide information and training;
    • provide eye tests on request for employees, and special spectacles if needed;
    • review the assessment when the user or equipment changes.
  • Stress: This is can be a major risk area. Consider using events like annual performance reviews to check whether people may be at risk. Consider the messages you give to managers about their duty to ensure people are not put under excessive strain, and what they should do if there appears to be a problem with an individual.
  • First aid: In larger workplaces it will normally be appropriate for some staff to have been trained in first aid. Also a minimum requirement is a suitably stocked first-aid box. Your risk assessment should indicate whether any additional materials and equipment are required such as scissors, microporous adhesive tape, disposable aprons and moist wipes.
  • Fire safety: You will normally want to check with your landlord as to which responsibilities are managed by the landlord and which by the tenant. Issues to consider include what fire extinguishers or fire blankets are required, signing of fire exits, appointing fire marshals and organising fire practices.
  • Working out of the office: If anyone is expected to travel or to work anywhere other than their office or home then your risk assessment and policy should cover associated risks. Take particular care if people travel to high-risk countries, or any location where they may be at extra risk.
  • Food safety: If your firm provides food for staff or meetings it is vital that the relevant manager has appropriate food safety training and follows appropriate procedures e.g. on disposal of food which may no longer be safe to eat.

 

COMMITMENT STATEMENT

To all staff.

We attach a copy of the firm’s health and safety policy. Please read and apply it.

At ANP Solicitors, we believe that nobody should suffer occupational illness or injury as a result of their work.  Furthermore we believe that all harm can be prevented and sustainable zero harm can be achieved. Everyone can and must go home safe everyday

In order to achieve this, we are committed to the following.

·     Taking personal responsibility for our own health and safety and for those around us.

·     A structured approach to risk management, where our work is assessed for health and safety risks and those risks are either eliminated or mitigated to an acceptable level.

·     Effective consultation and engagement.

·     Taking the time to do every job safely, no matter how important or urgent it is.

·     Demonstrating visible leadership and accountability for safety and health when supervising people’s work.

·     Learning from experience and using this information to reduce the chance of harm in the future.

·     Encouraging open reporting of all incidents.

·     Identifying, reporting and dealing with hazards to help create a harm free workplace.

·     Providing all the necessary health and safety training and competent advice for employees to enable them to stay safe at work.

·     Providing leadership to ensure that these commitments are delivered.

We cannot achieve zero harm without your full support, so get involved, take responsibility, and ensure standards are met.

ANP SOLICITORS HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY

This is the statement of the general health and safety policy and arrangements for ANP Solicitors.

Responsibilities

The firm’s COLP for the time being has overall responsibility for health and safety, including for assessing risks, carrying out an annual review and monitoring standards.

The Directors have day-to-day responsibility for ensuring this policy is put into practice.

Statement of General Policy:

The firm seeks to ensure, so far as it can, that everyone in its offices has a safe environment in which to work, free from avoidable risks. The firm has concluded that it is generally a low risk environment, and is not aware of any unusual hazards to health and safety. However, no environment is risk free. Accordingly the firm’s policy is to do the following.

·     Manage health and safety risks to prevent accidents and cases of work-related ill health. To this end risk assessments are carried out at appropriate times and actions arising out of those assessments are implemented.

·     Provide clear instructions, information and training to employees.

·     Engage and consult with employees as necessary on health and safety conditions.

·     Implement emergency procedures, particularly for evacuation in case of fire or other significant incident. To this end escape routes are kept well signed and clear. Evacuation plans are tested from time to time and updated as necessary.

·     Maintain safe and healthy working conditions, provide and maintain plant, equipment and machinery, and ensure safe storage/use of substances.

The Role of All Staff

All staff are expected to play their part in reducing any health and safety risks. In particular you should do the following.

  • Take care for your own safety and that of others, including visitors.
  • Follow policies and instructions designed to maintain health and safety. For example the firm imposes restrictions on electrical items that may be brought into and used in the office and a failure to comply will be considered a disciplinary matter.
  • Report any hazards of which you are aware, including if you believe you or others are under unacceptable levels of stress.
  • Take part in any training or exercises the firm may organise.
