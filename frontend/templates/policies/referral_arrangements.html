REFERRAL ARRANGEMENTS

 

PRACTICE NOTE

  1. The Rules Now Apply to Lawyer Referrals[1]

Unlike the rules which applied before October 2011, Chapters 6 and 9 of the SRA Code of Conduct apply to referral arrangements with other lawyers.

  1. Referring Clients to Third Parties

2.1       Arrangements with Third Parties to Make Referrals

See the precedent clause, below, for use if your firm enters into any arrangement with a third party under which you will refer clients to them.  It is not mandatory for such arrangements to be in writing, but is clearly sensible.

Under SRA Code of Conduct Chapter 6 (to which you should refer) any such arrangement should comply with certain criteria.  In particular you must (with limited exceptions) avoid an arrangement which restricts your freedom to recommend any particular business.

2.2       Making a Referral

Any referral must comply with Chapter 6 of the SRA Code of Conduct.  That includes a referral to another lawyer, insurer, financial services provider etc., whether made pursuant to a previous arrangement with the third party, or ad hoc.  The main requirements are these.

·       Your recommendation must be in the best interests of the client, and must not compromise your independence.

·       Clients must be fully informed of any financial or other interest which you have in referring the client, and generally must be in a position to make informed decisions.

·       You must not be paid a prohibited referral fee.  Referral fees in personal injury cases are banned by s56 Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012.

Section 3.8 in the Socrates Risk Management Manual reminds staff of their obligations. [2]  The Socrates precedent Engagement Letter also has a specimen clause by which you may explain the position to clients.

  1. Referrals From Third Parties

See the precedent clause, below, for use if you enter into any arrangement with a third party under which you they may refer clients to your firm.

Under SRA Code of Conduct Chapter 9 any such arrangement should comply with criteria including these.

·       Any financial arrangement with an introducer is in writing.

·       Your independence and your professional judgement are not prejudiced, and your clients' interests are protected regardless of the interests of an introducer or fee sharer or your interest in receiving referrals.

·       Clients are in a position to make informed decisions.  They are informed of any financial or other interest which an introducer has in referring the client to you, and of any fee sharing arrangement.

·       You do not make payments to an introducer in respect of criminal or publicly funded clients.

·       You do not pay a prohibited referral fee.  Referral fees in personal injury cases are banned by s56 Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012.

The “Referrals Warning Guidance” from the SRA says “We are cracking down on solicitors whose referral arrangements compromise their clients' interests, and who undermine public confidence in solicitors.”  The SRA “Referrals Warning Guidance” is at www.sra.org.uk/referrals

 

PRECEDENT CLAUSES[3]

These clauses should be included in agreements under which you will refer clients to third parties, or under which clients will be referred to you.

Introductions to Third Parties[4]

“We must make it clear that referrals will only be made under this agreement where this is in the best interests of the particular client, and the [contract] is suitable for their needs.  Any such referral will not compromise our independence.  If you can only offer products from one source we will only make a referral to you after the client has been informed of this limitation, and we expect you also to explain that limitation to any client we refer to you.[5]  [This agreement does not restrict our freedom to recommend any other business.][6]  We will fully inform clients of any financial or other interest which we have in referring the client to you.”

Fee Sharing and Referrals From Third Parties[7]

“We have drawn your attention to our regulatory obligations, notably the SRA Principles and the SRA Code of Conduct.   You agree that this agreement is subject to those obligations, and to any later replacement or variation of them.  In particular you agree that:

·       this agreement does not prejudice our independence and professional judgement[8]

·       clients’ interests must be protected regardless of both your interests and of our interest in receiving referrals[9]

·       you will not be able to influence the advice we give to clients[10]

·       we will advise clients if any arrangement or proposed arrangement they have with you is not in their best interests[11]

·       to the extent that we and you jointly market services, we will both make clear the nature of the services provided by each of us[12]

·       you will not conduct publicity for us in a way that would breach the SRA Principles, or the SRA Code of Conduct, and in particular you will not make unsolicited approaches in person or by telephone to members of the public in order to publicise our services or to find clients who may be referred to us, and you accept that we will decline to act for any client who appears to have been approached in breach of this requirement[13]

·       we will not accept referrals where we have reason to believe that clients have been pressurised or misled into instructing us[14]

·       we will ensure our clients are in a position to make informed decisions, in particular by informing them in writing of any financial interest you have in referring them to us, and of any fee sharing arrangement between you and us, and drawing their attention to any payments we make or other consideration we provide in connection with any referral[15]

·       we will not make payments to you in respect of clients who are the subject of criminal proceedings or who have the benefit of public funding,[16] nor will we pay prohibited referral fees relating to personal injury claims[17]

·       we will monitor the outcome of this arrangement to ensure clients are treated fairly and you will give us such information as we request to enable us to do so[18]

·       we will terminate this arrangement if it is causing us to breach the SRA Principles or any requirement of the SRA Code of Conduct, and we reserve the right so to do without notice.[19]


[1] Apart from the fact that the rules now apply to referrals between lawyers, the SRA Code of Conduct provisions on referral arrangements are generally less restrictive than the rules which applied before 6 October 2011.

[2] In addition to the requirements of the SRA Code of Conduct, Lexcel v6 section 6.1 states  “Practices must have a policy for client care, including… a procedure for referring clients to third parties”.  This section of the Risk Management Manual should meet that need.

[3] If you own the third party (or have a significant interest in it, or actively participate in it, or are connected with it through common ownership etc.) then additional requirements apply under Chapter 12 of the SRA Code of Conduct.  For example Indicative Behaviour 12.3 is “where you are referring a client to a permitted separate business, informing the client of your interest in the separate business”.

[4] It is not mandatory to put such arrangements in writing, but of course it is sensible to do so.  For the detailed requirements see Chapter 6 of the SRA Code of Conduct. In particular note Outcome 6.2 which is that “clients are fully informed of any financial or other interest which you have in referring the client to another person or business”.  Your engagement letter should include a template for such disclosure.

[5] Indicative Behaviour 6.2 is (from 1 January 2013) is “any referral to a third party that can only offer products from one source, is made only after the client has been informed of this limitation.”

[6] If the arrangement is in respect of regulated mortgage contracts, general insurance contracts or pure protection contracts this sentence may be deleted.  See the SRA Code of Conduct Indicative Behaviour 6.3.

[7] For the detailed requirements see Chapter 9 of the SRA Code of Conduct.  Note in particular that any financial arrangement with an introducer must be in writing (Outcome 9.7).  You must inform clients of (i) any financial or other interest which an introducer has in referring the client to you and (ii) any fee sharing arrangement that is relevant to their matter (Outcomes 9.4 and  9.5).  Your engagement letter should include a template for such disclosure.
